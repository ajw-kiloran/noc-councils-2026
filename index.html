England's hung councils — May 2026

Coalition arithmetic and viability for English councils ending in no overall control after May 7, 2026.

⚠ Preliminary data. Counts were declared 7–8 May; some recounts and one postponed ward (Bradford – Idle & Thackley) may shift exact figures. Confidence labels per council reflect source agreement. Verify against the council's own official declaration before quoting.

Methodology

Coalition viability is scored from a pairwise compatibility matrix (Lab–LD = 0.78, Lab–Green = 0.80, Lab–Con = 0.22, Con–Reform = 0.50, Lab–Reform = 0.06 etc.) and the lowest pair score determines the overall floor. A coalition needs (a) seats ≥ majority threshold and (b) a viability floor ≥ 0.45 to be flagged green/medium. Independents and Other are scored as wildcards (default 0.55). Reform UK's national position has been to reject formal pacts with Conservatives, which is reflected in the score; observed local pacts may differ.

Kingmaker analysis. For each council we enumerate every minimum-winning coalition (one where every party is critical — remove any and the coalition loses). The "pivotal frequency" of each party is the number of viable MWCs it appears in. The kingmaker is the smaller party that appears in the most viable MWCs. A council is flagged "co-pivotal" when three or more parties are equally pivotal (any pair forms a majority — Cambridge is the canonical example). The dashboard also flags councils where a viable bloc can form a majority excluding the largest party — typically when the 2nd and 3rd parties combined clear the threshold.

Caveats & gaps

Lewisham (Green mayor + Labour-led chamber majority) and Tower Hamlets (Aspire mayor + chamber majority retained) were declared late and are not NOC. Croydon was added once the count concluded — only one of the three late-declaring boroughs ended in NOC. Bury, Rushmoor, Cannock Chase, Epping Forest, Swindon and Bradford figures are flagged LOW/MED confidence — totals do not always reconcile across sources. All English district councils on this list are scheduled for abolition in 2028 under Local Government Reorganisation; this universally constrains how ambitious any new administration can be.
